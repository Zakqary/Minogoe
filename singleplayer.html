How to Play

  • One random piece at a time (70% pentomino, 20% tetromino, 10% tromino). Your next 3 pieces are shown ahead.
  • Enclose a pocket of 4 or fewer empty squares to clear it and free the space.
  • Clear the whole board to finish. Fastest times make the leaderboard.
  • No legal placement for your current piece ends the run early.
  • 10×10 board, one random piece at a time, no preview.
  • Pieces never disappear. Any fully enclosed pocket counts as captured territory, no matter the size.
  • Goal is the opposite of a real match: keep captured territory as low as possible.
  • Run ends when your piece has no legal spot. Lowest captured wins.
  • Same rules as Eogonim, but placed pieces vanish from view instantly.
  • They still occupy their squares. Remember where you put them.
  • You'll never get the same shape twice in a row.
  • Clicking an occupied square ends your run immediately.
  • The board reveals fully once your run ends.
  • 10×10 board, played in rounds. Pick 1 of 3 shapes to start, with unlimited supply.
  • Capturing works like Eogonim: no removal, any pocket size counts.
  • Each round has a rising target (10, then 15, then 18, +2 after that).
  • Clear it to unlock a new shape and reset the board. Miss it and the run ends. Score is rounds cleared.
  • 10×10 board, one random piece at a time, no preview. Starts with 5 dead squares already on the board.
  • One more square dies (at random, never inside territory you've already captured) after every piece you place.
  • Dead squares can't be placed on, and they poison capturing - a pocket bordering one never counts, even if your own pieces surround the rest of it.
  • Run ends when your piece has no legal spot. Highest captured wins.
