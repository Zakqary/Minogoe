How to Play

  • You get one random piece at a time — 70% pentomino (5 squares), 20% tetromino (4), 10% tromino (3). Rotate/flip it however you like before placing.
  • You can see your next 3 upcoming pieces, so plan your board space ahead of time instead of leaving it to luck.
  • Place it anywhere it legally fits on the board.
  • Fully enclose a pocket of 4 or fewer empty squares and it's captured forever — the pieces that walled it in are cleared away, freeing that space back up. Bigger enclosures don't count, so you can't just wall off the whole board at once.
  • Clear every square on the board to finish your run — the timer stops and your time is saved if it's your best.
  • If your current piece has no legal placement anywhere, the run ends early. Fastest completed times appear on the leaderboard.
  • Smaller 8×8 board. You get one random piece at a time — same 70% pentomino / 20% tetromino / 10% tromino odds as Speedrun, and any of them can come up.
  • Unlike Speedrun, you don't get a preview — only the piece you must place right now is shown.
  • Fully enclose a pocket of 4 or fewer empty squares and it's captured — the pieces that walled it in are cleared away, and those squares count against your score.
  • The goal is the opposite of Speedrun: keep your total captured squares as low as possible.
  • The run ends the moment your current piece has no legal placement anywhere — that's just how every run ends, not a failure. Your final captured count is your score.
  • Lowest total captured squares wins a spot on the Golf leaderboard.
