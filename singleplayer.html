How to Play

  • You get one random piece at a time — 70% pentomino (5 squares), 20% tetromino (4), 10% tromino (3). Rotate/flip it however you like before placing.
  • You can see your next 3 upcoming pieces, so plan your board space ahead of time instead of leaving it to luck.
  • Place it anywhere it legally fits on the board.
  • Fully enclose a pocket of 4 or fewer empty squares and it's captured forever — the pieces that walled it in are cleared away, freeing that space back up. Bigger enclosures don't count, so you can't just wall off the whole board at once.
  • Clear every square on the board to finish your run — the timer stops and your time is saved if it's your best.
  • If your current piece has no legal placement anywhere, the run ends early. Fastest completed times appear on the leaderboard.
  • 10×10 board. You get one random piece at a time — same 70% pentomino / 20% tetromino / 10% tromino odds as Speedrun, and any of them can come up.
  • Unlike Speedrun, you don't get a preview — only the piece you must place right now is shown.
  • This is scored like regular Minogoe, not Speedrun: pieces never disappear. An empty pocket fully enclosed by your own pieces (and/or the board edge) counts as captured territory, no matter how big it is.
  • The goal is the opposite of a real match: keep your total captured territory as low as possible.
  • The run ends the moment your current piece has no legal placement anywhere — that's just how every run ends, not a failure. Your final captured total is your score.
  • Lowest total captured territory wins a spot on the Eogonim leaderboard.
  • 10×10 board, same one-random-piece-at-a-time draw as Eogonim — no preview of what's coming.
  • The twist: the instant you place a piece, it disappears from view. The board only ever shows what's currently in your hand — nothing you've already placed.
  • Those pieces are still there, occupying their squares, even though you can't see them anymore — you have to remember where you put them.
  • Click a square that's actually occupied (visible or not) and your run ends immediately as an illegal move.
  • Capturing works exactly like Eogonim — any fully enclosed empty pocket counts as captured territory, of any size. Running out of legal placements (or filling the board) ends the run normally too, same as Eogonim.
  • Once the run is over, the whole board is revealed so you can see exactly where everything was.
  • Lowest total captured territory wins a spot on the Blind Eogonim leaderboard.
  • 10×10 board, played in escalating rounds. Pick 1 of 3 random shapes to start — that's your only piece, in unlimited supply.
  • Pieces are drawn randomly from whichever shapes you've unlocked, and never disappear once placed — same enclosure scoring as Eogonim, any size pocket counts.
  • Each round has a target amount of territory to capture before you get stuck. Targets start at 10 and climb every round.
  • Clear a round's target and you'll pick a new shape to add to your collection, the board resets empty, and the next round begins.
  • Fail to hit a round's target before getting stuck and the run ends. Your leaderboard score is how many rounds you cleared.
